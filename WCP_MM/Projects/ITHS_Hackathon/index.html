Education

Invest in Video Games: Kids enjoy playing videos games and if the video games are good, they will keep on playing them.
Including video games in education is a powerful tool to learn more. Kids tend to remember stuff that happens in video games
more than things that take place in a classroom and the main reason for that is the interaction people have in video games and
how engaged they feel while playing.

I’ve met people that can tell me almost everything they saw in a video game they started playing hours ago but they can’t tell me
what did they learn in their class today. This is a perfect example to prove that video games helps people learn more. I’m not saying
that classrooms should be replaced by videos, my point is that video games should be included in education to make people learn in a
different way that might benefit more students.

· More educational websites: Websites have been around since the beginning of when computers were popularized. More often than not now,

Clean water for low income families and poor people: An enormous amount of people doesn’t have access to clean or enough water.
They get diseases and sicknesses that hinder the growth of their well being. Although technology can help people get clean water.
Border Project states “Such advances are not out of reach and modern technology can create achievable goals for water and sanitation.
Practical Action, for example, partnered with Kenyans from the dry, arid Turkana region to develop a solution to the area’s drought problems.”

“We developed a solar-powered water pump that uses locally-sourced equipment to pump 30,000
clean liters of clean and safe water to the village every day” the organization
reported. “So with technology at our side, we could bring clean and fresh water
to those in need. Soon enough we might be able to end this water crisis in the
world with technology”.
